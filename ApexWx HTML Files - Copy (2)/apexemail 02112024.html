The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Updated: 11 February 2024 at 9:40 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Sun. Feb. 11 -  Tue.  Feb. 13

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

A cold front will approach today and cross the region tonight producing mostly cloudy to overcast skies with snow showers, mixing with rain showers in the afternoon, before changing to all snow and ending this evening. The front will slowly move towards the Downeast coast by Monday night. A trough over the Crown of Maine will keep a chance for snow showers for Fort Kent and vicinity through the day Monday drier air moves in aloft ending precipitation Monday evening. Partly cloudy skies with much colder temperatures arrive Monday night and Tuesday with partly cloudy to partly sunny skies across the region.

Low pressure in the Lower Mississippi Valley today will track towards the Mid-Atlantic region then offshore by Monday night. Almost all models indicate that this system will slide along the southern edge of the Gulf of Maine and south of Nova Scotia by Tuesday. Currently, only the Downeast coast, particularly Washington and Hancock counties, will see about 2-4 inches of snowfall with no impact on the County.

4-7 Day Outlook: Wed. Feb. 14 - Sat. Feb. 17

Tuesday's low intensifies as it tracks towards Newfoundland. The region will be on the western periphery of the system's circulation. Windy conditions with some isolated snow showers are possible Wednesday and Thursday with mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies across the Saint John Valley. Winds diminish Friday, though remaining brisk with colder air across the region. Partly cloudy to partly sunny skies are expected Friday and Saturday. Long-range models indicate some Arctic air moving towards the region as we enter the second half of February later next week.
