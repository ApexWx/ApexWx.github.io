4-7 Day Outlook: Mon. Feb. 12 - Thu. Feb. 15

For the start of the week, a cold front will slowly slide towards the Downeast coast Monday with some isolated to scattered snow showers across northern Maine. Meanwhile, low pressure slides off the Mid-Atlantic and tracks northeast south of Cape Cod/Nova Scotia has it strengthens. While the German global forecast model suggests a more significant impact on Maine, the other forecast models have significant snowfall only affecting coastal Maine, so at the current time, some snow if expected Monday night into Tuesday morning then isolated snow showers Tuesday afternoon though Wednesday.

The low will continue to move east; however, it will deepen and produce gusty north winds across the County Wednesday with mostly cloudy skies across the region and colder air settling back into the Valley. An upper low will approach Wednesday into Thursday as it gets caught up in the circulation around the Atlantic low (east of Newfoundland) producing continued blustery winds and snow showers for Fort Kent and vicinity.
