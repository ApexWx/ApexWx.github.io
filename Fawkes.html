7-Day Outlook: Fri. July 25 - Thu. July 31
Updated July 25 at 9:33 am EDT

Friday -  Sunday
Low pressure slides by to the north on Friday as a cold front moves through late Friday night. This front will meander near the area on Saturday, before lifting back north as a warm front on Sunday. Another cold front will approach the area later Sunday ahead of high pressure returning for  Monday. Yet, another front appears to loom on the horizon around midweek.

Today will be sunny in the morning then partly cloudy/partly sunny in the afternoon with temperatures in the low 90s and heat indices ("feels like") topping out near 103¬∞ thanks to dew points in the 70s. A chance of afternoon showers and thunderstorms produce less than 1/10-inch rainfall, though locally higher totals possible in any storms. West wind 5-10 mph. Tonight, a slight chance of evening showers/thunderstorms with partly cloudy skies with lows in the the lower 70s and northwest wind 0-5 mph expected shifting to the north/northeast towards morning.

On Saturday, a weak disturbance moving around the northern edge of the high-pressure area is expected to cause several rounds of showers and thunderstorms along a wavy, stationary, or warm front into Saturday night. Recent forecasts suggest that the number of storms may be lower than previously expected, with some signs that much of Saturday could remain dry for most of the region, with Lancaster receiving less than 1/10-inch rainfall, unless any thunderstorms happen to produce more.

For Saturday night, a complex of showers and storms will continue moving eastward along the front, bringing a higher chance of rain, especially in eastern Pennsylvania. There is some uncertainty about how long this system will last or if it will weaken as it moves toward the coast. Because of this, the chance of rain is highest (around 50 to 60 percent) in the far western areas, such as Berks County and the Poconos, and lower farther east.

Sunday looks like it will be the more unsettled of the two weekend days. Showers and storms may continue into Sunday morning due to the complex moving in on Saturday night, which could last into Sunday. After that, there might be a break in the rain by late morning and early afternoon, but more showers and storms are expected to develop later in the day as the front remains in the area.

Since there may not be much of a break between the two rounds of rain, it looks like there will be little sunshine on Sunday, which will help keep temperatures and instability a bit lower. Because of this, the main concern for hazardous weather will be heavy rain and the potential for localized flash flooding. The rain could be widespread, with moisture levels high, which increases the risk of flooding

Monday - Thursday
The weather for the coming week will stay seasonably warm, with humid conditions continuing through the middle of the week. To start the week, high pressure will take control of the area, keeping it dry as it sits between two weather systems. This will likely be the driest day, with mostly sunny skies.

As the week goes on, temperatures and humidity will gradually rise, leading to a higher chance of afternoon showers and thunderstorms, especially to the north and west of the city. By Wednesday, a cold front is expected to move into the region and pass through by Wednesday night.

Forecast data suggests that a change in the weather pattern is likely to develop by the end of the period, around the beginning of August. This change involves a shift in the upper-level winds, with high pressure building northward over the Rocky Mountains and a low-pressure system moving south from Canada across the Great Lakes and the Northeast. As a result, more comfortable and pleasant weather conditions are expected to return after a cold front passes through on Thursday.
