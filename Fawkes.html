Thursday -  Saturday
A stalled cold front across the area will begin to move south of the area today. A cold front will approach from the north later today and cross the Lower Susquehanna Valley tonight with a slight chance of evening showers and thunderstorms. Strong to severe thunderstorms with gusty winds and small hail are possible this afternoon and early this evening. Dry high pressure will build into the region on Friday and remain in control through the weekend, providing seasonable temperatures and tranquil weather conditions. Warmer, more humid, and unsettled conditions will return early next week.

For today, mostly sunny skies with a slight chance of showers/thunderstorms this evening. West winds 5-10 mph with a high in the mid-to-upper 80s. Overnight, mostly clear skies with northwest wind 0-5 mph and lows in the lower 60s.

Independence Day looks sunny with highs in the low-to-mid-80s and northwest winds 5-6 mph. Friday night, mostly clear skies over Fawkes House with lows in the lower 60s and near calm winds.

Saturday, mostly sunny skies with temperatures in the mid-80s. South winds near calm increasing to 3-4 mph in the afternoon. Saturday night, mostly clear with lows in the mid-60s and light south winds.
