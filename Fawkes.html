3-Day Outlook: Tue. May 6 - Thu. May 8

An upper-level low will slowly move northeastward and cross the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic regions by Tuesday night, then it will lift out during Wednesday. A cold front will cross the area Wednesday night and Thursday morning. Another upper-level low develops across the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic regions Thursday night and Friday with weak low pressure nearby, then it shifts northeast on Saturday.

4-7 Day Outlook: Fri. May 9 - Mon. May 12

The closed low will settle across the Mid-Atlantic on Friday, supporting the development of low pressure along or just off the New Jersey coast. Showers will continue to develop through the day with a few embedded thunderstorms as well. Some guidance suggesting a period of rain could be possible, though there remains a bit of model spread regarding the coverage and intensity of rainfall for Friday.

The low-pressure system is expected to weaken and move out of the region on Saturday, but some unsettled weather may still linger. Conditions will start to dry out, and temperatures will gradually warm up as the system moves away.

On Sunday, a weather system from eastern Canada will bring a cold front through our area. Right now, it looks like there won’t be much moisture or strong support for rain, so any showers are expected to be light and scattered.

By Monday, high pressure will move in, bringing warmer and drier weather. A sea breeze may develop in the afternoon due to light winds. Monday night, the high may shift offshore, and some models suggest a possible system off the Southeast U.S. coast, which may bring some moisture to the area.
