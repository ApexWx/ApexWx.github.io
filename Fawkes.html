Surface high pressure brings mostly sunny skies today with some clouds possible in the afternoon thanks to an upper trough just to the east near the PA/NJ border. Partly cloudy skies tonight and only a very slight chance for showers. today's high reaches the middle 70s with a low tonight in the mid-50s.

Mostly sunny skies are expected Tuesday with a high in the mid-70s. Mostly clear skies spread across the Lower Susquehanna Valley Tuesday night with Lancaster's low falling into the middle 50s.

Wednesday will be warmer with high pressure moving offshore and southwest flow setting up at the surface behind the ridge. Mostly clear skies with southwest winds are expected Wednesday night. Wednesday's high rises towards the middle 80s with overnight temperatures in the low 60s.

4-7 Day Outlook: Thu. June 13 - Sun. June 16

Upper flow should become more zonal for the second half of the week. At the surface, a strong high pressure system will park over Pennsylvania Wednesday into Friday and promote clear skies, calm winds, and increasingly-mild temperatures. Highs will be in the mid-70s to mid-80s on Wednesday, which is just about average for this time of year. It looks like Thursday and Friday will be downright summer-like, with highs topping 80 degrees area-wide and even approaching or topping the 90-degree mark in the valleys of south-central Pennsylvania.

The next chance of showers and storms will be Friday, as a cold front drops southeastward from the Great Lakes. High pressure should build back in for next weekend, with a brief cool down expected on Saturday before temperatures begin another upward trend on Sunday.
