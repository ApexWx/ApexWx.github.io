7-Day Outlook: Thu. June 5 - Wed. June 11
Updated: Thursday, 5 June 2025 at 9:05 am EDT

Uneventful weather during the near term with high pressure well offshore and subsidence within the local region. The nearest disturbance is a cold front back across the upper Great Lakes, however a weak coastal system is also taking shape along the Carolina coast. The front will creep slowly east through the day today, and the coastal system will slowly move north along the coast through today as well. Unlike the past couple days though, high cloud cover from the approaching systems will blanket the region, especially by this afternoon.

The cold front will inch closer on Friday. Its progress still looks to be slow with little upper-level support and the continued presence of the coastal surface low just south of the region. Most forecast models have the coastal low moving out to sea Friday night in the waking of the departing high. With the cold front coming in from the west and being a bit closer, showers and thunderstorms are possible by later Friday afternoon and evening. A chance of showers remains possible overnight into Saturday morning.

By Saturday, with an upper-level trough beginning to deepen to our west and the coastal low moving further offshore, the cold front will finally have the support to begin to move across the area, and lead to increasing chances of precipitation, with showers likely Saturday along with scattered, mainly afternoon, thunderstorms.

High pressure might briefly return on Sunday, but another weak weather system could push a stalled front back north into the area. Because of this, there is now a 30‚Äì40% chance of showers or a thunderstorm across much of the region on Sunday. After this system moves out, high pressure is expected to return late Sunday into early Monday. However, another system could bring more rain later on Monday.

By Tuesday, a larger system will start moving in. This will allow a warm front to move north through the region, followed by a cold front on Wednesday. Showers and a few thunderstorms are likely on Tuesday and Wednesday, but rain chances should decrease later Wednesday as the cold front moves out.
