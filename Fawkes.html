7-Day Outlook: Fri. May 15 - Thu. May 22

A warm front lifts north across the area this morning. Periods of showers, thunderstorms, and humidity continue through Saturday ahead of a strong cold front that passes through late Saturday afternoon into Saturday evening. High pressure builds into the area on Sunday and persists into Tuesday before another period of unsettled weather returns Wednesday into Thursday.

This afternoon's thunderstorm setup is a bit uncertain because there's no strong weather system to trigger storms. However, conditions are still favorable for storm development. There will be plenty of heat and moisture in the air, with temperatures rising due to breaks in the clouds and dew points (a measure of moisture) in the mid-60s to near 70¬∞F across south-central Pennsylvania ‚Äî similar to what occurs in mid-summer.

Winds higher up in the atmosphere are weak, so the main concerns are likely to be isolated strong wind gusts, hail, and heavy downpours. These downpours could cause localized flooding, especially in areas that have already seen a lot of rain over the past few days.

A cold front will move through the area by Saturday evening, bringing scattered showers and a few thunderstorms. The best chance for stronger storms will be in the eastern part of the region, where conditions look more favorable. Some of these storms could produce strong winds, especially if the atmosphere becomes more unstable.

After the front passes, the weather will turn cooler and windier for the rest of the weekend. Sunday is expected to be breezy, with frequent wind gusts between 25 and 35 mph. Temperatures will drop about 10 degrees from Saturday, with highs ranging from the mid-60s to mid-70s.

Dry weather will continue through early next week, with fair skies expected through Tuesday afternoon thanks to a high-pressure system. Clouds will increase later on Tuesday, especially in the Laurel Highlands, as a new weather system approaches. Temperatures on Monday and Tuesday will be slightly cooler than normal, especially during the day.

There‚Äôs still some uncertainty about how this next system will develop. Some weather models suggest the rain will not arrive until Wednesday afternoon or evening, while others show it moving in earlier, possibly Tuesday night into Wednesday morning. Because of these differences, the forecast leans toward a later arrival of rain. Once the rain begins, showers are expected to continue into the end of the week as the system lingers and keeps moisture in the area.
