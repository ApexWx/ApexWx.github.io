The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Updated: February 9, 2024 at 8:50 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Sat. Feb. 10 -  Mon.  Feb. 12

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

A warm front will cross the area today as low pressure tracks well to our north. High pressure will build in Sunday into Monday. Low pressure will track south of the area Tuesday and continue south of the Maritimes on Wednesday. The storm will deepen significantly as it moves offshore on Tuesday and the northern shield of precipitation will brush Downeast. No snow looks likely in the northern half of the forecast area. Snow showers in Aroostook County will become more of a risk Wednesday into Thursday as a cold upper low seems likely to take up residence over the region. This threat would be highest in northern zones.

Sunday night: Mostly cloudy with a 40% chance for snow showers. Low near 20. Northwest wind 4-5 mph.

4-7 Day Outlook: Tue. Feb. 13 - Fri. Feb. 16

A potential storm for next Tuesday. A southern stream closed upper low loaded with deep moisture will emerge from the lower Mississippi Valley on Monday. The question over recent days was how this system would phase with the northern stream trough descending southward from the Great Lakes region Monday night into Tuesday.
