3-Day Outlook: Fri. June 14 - Sun. June 16

Current NWS Caribou, ME Hazardous Weather Outlook

A cold front will approach this morning with a chance of scattered showers across the Valley this morning into the afternoon. Showers and scattered thunderstorms are expected this afternoon into the evening. Isolated strong-to-sever storms are possible, mainly in southern Maine, with non-severe storms expected for the Saint John Valley. Gusty south/southwest winds are expected for the Saint John Valley as well today. Daytime highs reach the mid-70s for most locations.

Partly cloudy skies overspread the Valley tonight with lows dipping into the upper 40s. High pressure near the Great Lakes builds east into the Valley Saturday and crests over the Sunday into Monday then moves southeast into the Atlantic Sunday into Monday. Daytime highs reach the middle 60s Saturday and fall into the lower 40s Saturday night under mostly clear skies.

Sunny skies with highs in the low 70s are expected Sunday with partly cloudy skies Sunday night and lows in the upper 40s Sunday night.

4-7 Day Outlook: Mon. June 17 - Thu. June 20

High pressure remains near the Gulf of Maine Monday with mostly sunny skies continuing across the Valley. Daytime highs reach the lower 80s with partly cloudy skies Monday night and lows in the upper 50s.

A subtropical high pressure ridge brings hot and humid air into the Saint John Valley Tuesday through Thursday. NWS Caribou notes that "[n]ear record high temperatures beginning Tuesday may bring heat indices into the middle 90s to near 100 through the middle part of the week. Record high minimum temperatures Tuesday night into Thursday night are also expected. This promises to be the biggest heat wave since 2020 and among the most significant for northern and eastern Maine so far this century."

Tuesday looks sunny, hot, and increasingly humid with a high in the low 90s and a slight chance of afternoon/evening showers. Partly cloudy skies are expected Tuesday night with a slight chance of showers. Overnight temperatures drop into the mid-60s.

Sunny, hot, and humid conditions continue Juneteenth with highs reaching the mid-90s. Once again, a slight chance of mainly afternoon/evening showers is possible. Wednesday night will see partly cloudy skies with temperatures dropping into the upper 60s along with a slight chance of showers.

Mostly sunny, hot, and humid conditions continue Thursday with high climbing into the low 90s with an approaching cold front bringing a chance of scattered showers to the SJV Thursday afternoon. A chance of showers continues Thursday night with overnight lows falling into the lower 60s.
