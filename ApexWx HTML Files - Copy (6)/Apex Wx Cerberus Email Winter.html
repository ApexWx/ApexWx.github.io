⚠️ Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook ⚠️

7-Day Outlook: Mon. June 9 - Sun. June 15
Updated: Monday, June 9, 2025 at 7:43 am EDT

Mostly sunny skies are expected today as high pressure drifts east of the region with southerly flow across the Valley. Daytime highs reach the upper 70s in Fort Kent and surrounding areas. Clouds increase tonight as an occluding frontal system approaches from the west with a slight chance of showers after midnight. Overnight temperatures in the low 50s are expected.

A large upper-level low over the Great Lakes will move northeast into Québec Tuesday. Cloudy skies spread across the SJV Tuesday with a chance of showers, especially in the afternoon/evening along with isolated thunderstorms across the area. There will be plenty of moisture associated with this system, so there is a marginal risk of excessive rainfall for Maine; however, the degree of atmospheric instability (and any thunderstorms that occur) will also influence the amount of rain that falls across the Valley Tuesday into Tuesday night. Clouds and rain will keep highs in the mid-60s Tuesday with overnight lows in the mid-50s.

For Wednesday, there will be a lull in activity in the morning as the occluded front moves east with a slight chance of showers behind that system. The chance of showers increases in the afternoon as a cold front approaches from the west with a surface low looking to track just north of Maine. The cold front looks to cross the region Wednesday night with showers likely overnight. Highs reach the mid-70s with lows in the mid-50s.

Partly sunny skies and showers are expected Thursday along with the possibility of isolated afternoon thunderstorms. Highs top out in the upper 60s. Partly cloudy skies with a slight chance of showers are on tap Thursday night with lows dipping into the middle 40s.

The weekend has some uncertainty in the forecast with regards to the location of a ridge of high pressure across the region. Currently, the American, Canadian, and European models are generally suggesting high pressure to the northwest at the start of the period that moves across the region Friday into Sunday. This scenario produces partly cloudy skies Friday (and a slight chance of an afternoon shower) and mostly clear skies Saturday and Saturday night with increasing cloudiness Sunday as a disturbance approaches.

However, is the ridge is centered further north, then there will be a higher chance of precipitation, though Downeast areas would be more likely to see precipitation, based on current model data. So, the late-week weather setup will bear monitoring in upcoming forecast model runs.
