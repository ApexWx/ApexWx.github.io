The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine

⚠️ Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook ⚠️

7-Day Outlook: Sun. July 27 - Sat. Aug. 2
Updated July 27 at 9:45 am EDT

Sunday -  Tuesday
High pressure will drift into the Atlantic southeast of Nova Scotia today with southwest flow on the backside of the high pressure ridge will produce mostly sunny and hot conditions Sunday with highs in the upper 80s. Southwest winds 5-10 mph with some gusts to around 20 mph at times. A weak upper trough moving through produces a chance of afternoon showers/thunderstorms, though storm coverage varies from isolated to scattered on high-resolution models.

Tonight, a chance of evening showers/thunderstorms wanes by midnight with partly cloudy skies overnight and any precipitation amounts expected to be less than 1/10-inch (unless storms produce locally higher totals). Temperatures in the lower 60s with northwest winds 0-5 mph expected.

Monday, high pressure at the surface and aloft will bring mostly sunny skies and somewhat cooler temperatures in the lower 80s for Fort Kent and surrounding communities. Another weak disturbance brings a chance of showers Monday night into Tuesday morning with overnight lows in the lower 60s and near calm winds.

Tuesday, party sunny to mostly cloudy skies overspread the Valley as a cold front moves through with a chance of showers and scattered thunderstorms for Fort Kent and vicinity. Highs in the low 80s with west/southwest winds early 6-8 mph shift to the west/northwest later 4-6 mph. Tuesday night, showers and isolated thunderstorms are possible with lows in the upper 50s and light northwest winds across the region as a secondary cold front approaches.

Wednesday - Saturday
The secondary cold front moves through Wednesday with a chance of scattered showers, mainly in the afternoon with scattered showers overnight into Thursday. A large upper low in northern Québec will bring partly cloudy skies and a slight chance of showers Thursday as the Valley will be near the southern edge of the low's circulation with some disturbances moving through the circulation aloft.

A large ridge of Canadian high pressure builds east into the region for Friday and Saturday with cooler temperatures, dry conditions, and mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies across the SJV. Daytime highs Friday top out in the low 70s with overnight lows in the middle 40s Friday night. Saturday features mostly sunny skies with highs in the mid-70s with a slight chance of afternoon showers/thunderstorms. A chance of showers/thunderstorms Saturday evening is followed by mostly clear skies Saturday night  with lows in the lower to middle 50s.

Storms, Flash Flooding, and Extreme Heat to Impact Large Parts of the U.S.
A strong weather system in the South is pushing moist air northward, where it will meet a storm system over the Upper Midwest. Thunderstorms are expected this afternoon in northern Minnesota, possibly forming a larger storm complex tonight. There is a slight risk of flash flooding and severe storms with damaging winds and hail. Scattered storms may also impact the Ohio Valley, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic today, where wet ground increases the flash flooding risk.
