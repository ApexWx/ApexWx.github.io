The Week Ahead by Apex Wx
Updated Monday, December 19 at 8:00 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

NWS Caribou has issued a Winter Storm Warning in effect until 1 pm EST today
Snow. Additional snow accumulations between 1 and 4 inches for a storm total of 12 to 20 inches. A Winter Storm Warning for snow means severe winter weather conditions will make travel dangerous. If you must travel, keep an extra flashlight, food, and water in your vehicle in case of an emergency. The latest road conditions for Maine can be obtained by going to newengland511.org.

The low pressure system that brought this weekend's snow will continue to move very slowly through the Maritimes through tonight while an upper-level trough of low pressure remains nearly stationary across eastern Maine. Snow is likely through the day into the evening hours before tapering to snow showers this evening. Fort Kent residents may see around 1-4 inches additional snowfall with less than one-inch of additional snowfall overnight tonight. Temperatures will remain relatively mild for this time of year behind the system.

High pressure will build across the regoin Tuesday through Thursday with mostly cloudy skies Tuesday and Tuesday night followed by partly sunny skies Wednesday and partly cloudy skies Wednesday night. A slight chance for isolated flurries/snow showers is possible both days.

4-7 Day Outlook: Thursday - Sunday

High pressure will exit east across the Maritimes Thursday. The weekend forecast is still rather uncertain, but models are indicating that a strong low pressure system ahead of an Arctic air mass will move towards the Northeast in the late week period. Both the American and Canadian models have the main low over the Great Lakes lifting to our northwest into Canada.

The system will pull an occluded front across the region with snow showers Friday morning becoming a steadier snow Friday afternoon then transitioning to rain overnight into early Saturday. Rain tapers to showers Saturday morning then tapers to snow showers Christmas Eve that linger overnight into Christmas morning.

This has the potential to be a high precipitation event with abundant rainfall; however, exact details are still uncertain this far out. A strong pressure gradient is also likely to be a factor with windy conditions Friday and Friday night with breezy conditions Saturday and Sunday for the Saint John Valley.
