The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Updated: March 3, 2024 at 8:30 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Sun. Mar. 3 -  Tue.  Mar. 5

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

Low pressure tracks from Cape Cod towards the southwestern tip of Nova Scotia bringing rainfall to Downeast areas today and a slight chance for isolated showers to the Valley today. High pressure builds in with partly cloudy skies tonight with a slight chance for showers after tonight as low-level moisture trapped beneath drier air higher aloft moves across the SJV. Partly sunny skies Monday become mostly cloudy Monday night as an area of low pressure in southern New England tracks north bringing a chance for rain overnight into Wednesday.

Monday night: Partly cloudy with a 5% chance for showers. Low near 36. South wind 5-10 mph.

Tuesday: Mostly cloudy with a 20% chance for rain. High around 48. South wind 10-15 mph gusting 20-25 mph.

Tuesday night: Mostly cloudy with a 50% chance for rain. Low near 38. South wind 8-14 mph.

4-7 Day Outlook: Wed. Mar. 6 - Sat. Mar. 9

Mostly cloudy skies are expected Wednesday with a chance for rain across the SJV. A chance for rain continues Wednesday night as a cold front from the west moves through. Model uncertainty is high for the late week period with the possibility for some snow across the Saint John Valley; however, due to model variations, precise details are not clear at this time. and only a slight chance of snow is currently projected for Thursday through Saturday under mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies.
