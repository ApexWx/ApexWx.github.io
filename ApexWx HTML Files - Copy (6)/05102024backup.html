The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Updated: May 10, 2024 at 8:20 am EDT → Visiting after this time? Check for an update from NWS Caribou, Maine.

3-Day Outlook: Fri. May 10 - Sun. May 12

Current NWS Caribou, ME Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information.

Friday: High pressure will build across the area today with mostly sunny skies this morning (with some patchy fog about) giving way to a cumulus cloud field developing around midday continuing into the afternoon thanks to some moisture at about 6400 feet (based on 6 am EDT Bufkit atmospheric sounding analysis). An isolated shower cannot be ruled out, but widespread precipitation is not anticipated. Highs look to reach the upper-50s to lower 60s. Mostly clear to partly cloudy skies are expected overnight with temperatures in the mid-to-upper 30s.

Saturday will see the upper-level trough beginning to shift eastwards, though mostly sunny skies with a slight chance for afternoon showers (thanks to daytime heating) will remain possible for Fort Kent and other Valley locations. Cool air aloft along with some moisture will once again allow clouds to form over the SJV and keep daytime highs in the mid-50s for Fort Kent and vicinity. Partly cloudy skies Saturday night with a slight chance for an isolated showers as an upper-level trough near the Great Lakes begins to shift east. Overnight lows look to remain in the mid-to-upper 30s.

For Sunday, the trough moves closer the region with skies increasingly cloudy which will keep highs mostly in the mid-50s. A slight chance for daytime heating-driven showers is possible in the afternoon. Sunday night remains partly-to-mostly cloudy with a slight chance for showers in Fort Kent and vicinity. Lows dip into the upper 30s/lower 40s.

Daily Summary

Today: Patchy fog before 9am. Otherwise, mostly sunny, with a high near 60. Northeast wind 3 to 6 mph.

Tonight: Partly cloudy, with a low around 37. Southeast wind 3 to 5 mph.

Saturday: Mostly sunny, with a high near 56. East wind 3 to 6 mph.

Saturday Night: Partly cloudy, with a low around 38. East wind 3 to 6 mph.

Sunday: Partly sunny, with a high near 57. East wind around 6 mph.

Sunday Night: Mostly cloudy, with a low around 39. South wind 3 to 5 mph.

4-7 Day Outlook: Mon. May 13 - Thu. May 16

An occluding low near James Bay Monday will pull a triple point low across the region Tuesday into Wednesday with increasing chances for showers Monday afternoon/evening. Showers are possible Monday night into Tuesday with showers likely Tuesday afternoon as the region will be in the warm sector of this system.

A chance for showers continues Tuesday night into Wednesday with scattered showers and partly sunny skies. While there may be enough energy aloft to produce thunderstorms, uncertainty with the timing of frontal passage makes any convection uncertain at this time.

High pressure looks to build east out of Ontario/Québec by Thursday with isolated showers activity possible under partly cloudy skies. Partly cloudy skies with a slight chance for showers continues Thursday night into Friday morning.

Daily Summary

Monday: A slight chance of showers after 2pm. Partly sunny, with a high near 65. South wind 6 to 10 mph, with gusts as high as 20 mph. Chance of precipitation is 20%.

Monday Night: A chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 45. Chance of precipitation is 40%.

Tuesday: Showers likely, mainly after 10am. Mostly cloudy, with a high near 65. Chance of precipitation is 60%.

Tuesday Night: A chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 45. Chance of precipitation is 50%.

Wednesday: A chance of showers. Partly sunny, with a high near 66. Chance of precipitation is 40%.

Wednesday Night: A chance of showers. Partly cloudy, with a low around 41. Chance of precipitation is 30%.

Thursday: A slight chance of showers. Mostly sunny, with a high near 67. Chance of precipitation is 20%.
