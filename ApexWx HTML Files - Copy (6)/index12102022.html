The Week Ahead from Apex Wx
Updated Saturday, December 17 at 9:40 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

NWS Caribou has issued a Winter Storm Advisory until 1 am EST Monday, Dec. 19
Snow expected. Total snow accumulations between 7 and 13- inches. A Winter Weather Advisory for snow means periods of snow will cause primarily travel difficulties. Expect snow covered roads and limited visibilities, and use caution while driving.The latest road conditions for Maine can be obtained by going to newengland511.org.

An intensifying area of low pressure in the Gulf of Maine will bring a winter storm to Maine this weekend. Drier air over northern portions of the County will keep snowfall rates rather light today and tonight. Wrap around moisture and an approaching upper-level low from the west will produce additional snowfall Sunday as a trough forms between the Gulf low and the upper-low tomorrow. The trough will lift north over the state Sunday then north of the region Monday.

The heaviest snowfall is expected to be in central Maine with Katahdin receiving up to 3-feet of snowfall. Fort Kent,  and Frenchville, St. Agatha and areas in the Allagash / North Woods are expected to receive between 7 and 13-inches. Since the expected snowfall totals will accumulate the expected totals over a couple of days (rather than a 24-hour period), this is the reason NWS Caribou is leaving the event at an advisory level. Nonetheless, the storm will still produce hazardous travel conditions, particularly for anyone needing to travel south in the state.

The upper-low will move east Monday with less than one-inch additional snow accumulations expected in its wake. Snow showers are expected overnight into Tuesday before ending early Tuesday morning.
