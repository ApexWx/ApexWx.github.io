NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Gusty winds will lead to some patchy blowing snow across Northern Maine later this morning into the evening. Some of the higher terrain could see winds gusting to 35 to 40 mph.
Short-Term Outlook: Friday - Sunday

Partly sunny to mostly cloudy skies continue today and Saturday as a low pressure system remains northeast of the Valley near the mouth of the Saint Lawrence. Some weak disturbances are expected to circulate around the upper-low associated with the system producing some snow showers through the period along with continued breezy winds. Some areas of blowing snow may occur with whiteouts possible, so practice due caution if driving. The low will finally move away Saturday night with decreasing winds and clouds for Saturday night and Sunday and warming temperatures as a high pressure ridge to the west moves east.

Medium-Range: Monday - Thursday

A large upper-level high pressure ridge will shift east early next week with surface high pressure sliding from the Great Lakes into the Southeast by Monday.  A warm front moving in from the west will bring some increased cloudiness to the Valley Tuesday with a chance for some snow showers. Wednesday will feature high pressure and partly cloudy skies in the morning with increased cloudiness in the afternoon as a warm front lifts towards the region followed by a cold front Thursday that may see some rain showers Thursday afternoon. The jet stream will orient southwest to northeast across the continental US channeling warm southerly air into the region as the week progresses.
