3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

A cold front will move across the Valley this morning and exit into the Maritimes this afternoon. Partly sunny to mostly cloudy skies this afternoon remain mostly cloudy this evening as an upper-level low to our northwest shifts eastward and a coastal low tracks toward the Maritimes.

Low pressure tracking along the coast tonight will bring a chance for snow as colder air is drawn into the Valley. Fort Kent and vicinity will be looking at less than one-inch of snow accumulation. Katahdin and vicinity may see 6-8 inches based on elevation.Snow tapers to snow showers Sunday morning before changing to rain as the low tracks into the Maritimes and temperatures warm as the morning progresses.

Rain continues into Sunday afternoon as a large upper-level low crosses the region then tapers to showers as the afternoon progresses. Breezy northwest winds will push wind chill values into the 30s making for a rather chilly day in Fort Kent and vicinity. Mostly cloudy skies are expected Sunday evening with rain showers ending by midnight and partly cloudy skies developing overnight.

High pressure builds across the SJV Monday with mostly sunny skies, light wind, and mild conditions. Clouds begin to increase overnight Monday night as the next system approaches the region.
