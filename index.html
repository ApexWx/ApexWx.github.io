Current NWS Caribou, ME Hazardous Weather Outlook

3-Day Outlook: Thu. Dec. 12 - Sat. Dec. 14

Wednesday-Thursday 12/11-12/12 rainfall total recorded by the Davis Vantage Pro 2 personal weather station was 2.11 inches. Less than 1/2-inch snowfall was recorded yesterday, 12/12.

After a stormy couple of days, cold and more tranquil weather is in store for the weekend.

Surface high pressure near the Great Lakes/Ohio Valley will build across the SJV today into Sunday with decreasing cloudiness today. Daytime highs reach the low 20s. However, an upper-level trough and disturbance moving through it will bring a chance of some flurries to Fort Kent and vicinity today. Breezy west winds with wind chill values around 0¬∞F will also be notable today.

Tonight, partly cloudy skies with lows dipping into the low teens are anticipated. Wind chill values to -5¬∞F are possible tonight. West winds 5-10 with gusts into the 20-25 mph range are possible in the evening.

Sunny skies and chilly conditions will prevail across the Valley Saturday with highs around 20¬∞F. Saturday night, clear skies and light west winds will allow radiational cooling to drop temperatures to around 5¬∞F in Fort Kent.

High pressure looks to crest over southern Maine Sunday with daytime highs under sunny skies reaching the mid-20s. Sunday night looks mostly clear with lows dipping into the mid-teens.

Daily Summary

Today: Scattered flurries after 10am. Partly sunny, with a high near 21. West wind 11 to 15 mph, with gusts as high as 26 mph.

Tonight: Partly cloudy, with a low around 10. Wind chill values as low as -1. West wind 8 to 11 mph, with gusts as high as 22 mph.

Saturday: Sunny, with a high near 20. Wind chill values as low as -1. Northwest wind 6 to 8 mph.

Saturday Night: Mostly clear, with a low around 5. Northwest wind around 5 mph becoming calm in the evening.

Sunday: Sunny, with a high near 25. Light southwest wind.

Sunday Night: Mostly clear, with a low around 14. Calm wind.

4-7 Day Outlook: Sun. Dec. 15 - Wed. Dec. 18

Mostly sunny skies and dry conditions are expected Monday with temperatures moderating into the lower 30s as high pressure drifts east of the region and southerly return flow around the ridge sets up. Overnight lows under partly cloudy skies Monday night fall into the lower 20s with light south winds.

A front approaches from the Great Lakes Tuesday/Wednesday with morning snow showers and afternoon rain then rain showers overnight into Wednesday morning. Rain/snow showers are then expected Wednesday morning as the front moves through. Highs in the low 40s Tuesday and the middle 30s Wednesday with overnight lows in the low 30s Tuesday night. Rainfall totals expected to be less than 1/2-inch should not create any hydrological issues with this system.

Surface high pressure centered over the Mid-Atlantic region will build in with mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies Wednesday into Thursday. A low pressure system approaches from the west late next week with a chance of snow for the Saint John Valley; however, track and timing variations for the system are found on current computer forecast models at this time.
