3-Day Outlook: Mon. June 3 - Wed. June 5

Current NWS Caribou, ME Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information.

Note: The Davis Vantage Pro 2 weather station rain gauge has failed after 15 years of service. A scientifically calibrated manual rain gauge will be utilized until a replacement sensor can be installed (sensor anticipated to arrive around June 6).

For today, occluding low pressure over the Maritimes moves south/southeast over the North Atlantic which will bring some cloudiness to the Saint John Valley as a back door cold front crossed the region overnight. On current radar imagery, a thin band of light shower activity is showing up along the Maine/New Brunswick border with the heaviest precipitation occurring in Washington County.

In Fort Kent, partly cloudy to partly sunny skies are expected with a slight chance of an isolated shower, with little precipitation, if any, anticipated. Daytime highs climbing into the mid-70s. Partly cloudy skies with an overnight low tonight in the lower 50s.

High pressure builds back into the Valley Tuesday with mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies.  Highs reach the middle 70s with partly cloudy skies expected Tuesday night with a low in the lower 50s.

Wednesday looks mostly sunny with temperatures climbing into the lower 80s followed by mostly clear skies Thursday night with temperatures falling into the mid-50s.

Daily Summary

Today: Partly sunny, with a high near 77. Northeast wind 5 to 8 mph.

Tonight: Partly cloudy, with a low around 50. East wind around 5 mph becoming calm in the evening.

Tuesday: Mostly sunny, with a high near 76. Light east wind.

Tuesday Night: Partly cloudy, with a low around 52. Calm wind.

Wednesday: Mostly sunny, with a high near 82. Light southeast wind.

Wednesday Night: Mostly clear, with a low around 56. Light southeast wind.

4-7 Day Outlook: Thu. June 6 - Sun June 9

High pressure begins to move east into the Atlantic Thursday while an occluding low pressure system slowly approaches from the west. An associated upper level low will close or become cutoff and looks to meander over the Middle Atlantic late week into the weekend.

With regards to sensible weather for Fort Kent and vicinity, showers are expected to develop across the Saint John Valley by Thursday evening with mostly cloudy skies and scattered to widely spread showers possible through the weekend.

NWS Caribou notes that the "best chance for rain currently appears to be on Saturday as a front pushes through, but all guidance has trended down on [chances of precipitation] and [amounts] for the late week into the weekend and it is not inconceivable that northern and eastern Maine receive little precipitation from the entire event."
