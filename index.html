Support NOAA/NWS!

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and the National Weather Service (NWS) play a vital role in protecting lives, property, and the environment. Their accurate forecasts, severe weather warnings, and climate monitoring support emergency responders, businesses, farmers, and the public every day.

By combining cutting-edge science, satellite technology, and data analysis, they help communities prepare for and respond to hazards like hurricanes, floods, and winter storms. As a forecaster, their data, analysis, and prognostics are essential elements of my daily forecast posts.

Their work is essential to national resilience and economic stability. Supporting NOAA and NWS means supporting science-driven decision-making and safeguarding our future in the face of changing weather and climate conditions. As extreme weather becomes more frequent, we need to strengthen—not weaken—our forecasting and preparedness systems.

Please advocate for robust support of NOAA and NWS in upcoming legislation by writing your senator and/or representative. Thank you.

3-Day Outlook: Thu. May 8 - Sat. May 10

High pressure centered near James Bay in Canada will build into the Valley today. Plenty of moisture aloft will keep mostly cloudy to overcast conditions across the region with cool temperatures for most Valley communities today. Mostly cloudy skies remain over the region tonight and Friday as high pressure drifts by to the north of the region.

An area of low pressure looks to develop along the Mid-Atlantic coast and slide towards Downeast Maine Friday into Saturday. Showers overspread the SJV Friday evening with rain likely Friday night and Saturday under overcast skies. Rain continues Saturday night tapering to showers Sunday morning as the low slides into the Maritimes.

Daily Summary

Today: Mostly cloudy, with a high near 49. North wind 9 to 13 mph, with gusts as high as 24 mph.

Tonight: Mostly cloudy, with a low around 34. North wind around 6 mph becoming calm in the evening.

Friday: Mostly cloudy, with a high near 57. Light northeast wind.

Friday Night: A chance of showers before midnight, then rain, mainly after midnight. Low around 43. Calm wind. Chance of precipitation is 80%. New precipitation amounts of less than a tenth of an inch possible.

Saturday: Rain. Patchy fog between 7am and 8am. High near 51. North wind 3 to 7 mph. Chance of precipitation is 80%. New precipitation amounts between a tenth and quarter of an inch possible.

Saturday Night: Rain likely before 10pm, then a chance of showers after 10pm. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 38. Northwest wind 5 to 7 mph. Chance of precipitation is 60%. New precipitation amounts between a tenth and quarter of an inch possible.


 4-7 Day Outlook: Sun. May 11 - Wed. May 14

A strong pressure difference between a departing low and a building high-pressure system may bring gusty northwest winds on Sunday. Winds could be stronger in higher terrain due to mixing during the day with gusts up to around 25 mph possible.

High pressure will move in from the south on Monday, with a potential upper-level disturbance bringing a chance of a shower to the Crown of Maine later in the day. Temperatures will begin to rise above normal starting Monday and will stay warm through the week. No major systems are expected to affect the SJV until the late week period based on current forecast model data.
