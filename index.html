3-Day Outlook: Friday - Sunday

NWS Caribou has issued a Frost Advisory from 12 midnight tonight until 8 am Saturday morning.

High pressure will continue to build down from the northwest as low pressure to the east/northeast moves away today. Gusty northwest winds this morning will subside this afternoon and evening as the high pressure ridge builds across New England. Clear skies and light winds will allow for frost to form in the western to central portions of northern Maine. Eastern portions of the County may retain wind a bit later into the evening, but patchy frost is still possible in sheltered/low-lying areas of the eastern part of the Valley overnight. NWS Caribou also notes that a "sharp low level inversion will also generate shallow fog in valleys and over the relatively warm rivers and lakes."

Saturday begins mostly clear with partly cloudy skies developing in the afternoon as a weak cold front drops down from the north. This will bring cloudiness overnight along with some shower activity for Fort Kent and vicinity with rainfall totals less than one-tenth an inch expected.

Lingering showers Sunday morning dissipate by midday with mostly sunny skies overspreading the Valley Sunday afternoon as high pressure builds down from the northwest once more. Light winds and clear skies Sunday night will bring another chance for frost to form across the region along with patchy river/lake fog.
