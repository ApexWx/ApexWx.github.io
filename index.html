3-Day Outlook: Sat. May 24 - Mon. May 26

Surface and upper-level low pressure will meander slowly east in New Brunswick while high pressure near the Great Lakes gradually builds eastwards towards the Northeast. Wrap-around showers will remain across the Saint John Valley making for a grey, showery day for northern Maine communities. Temperatures are expected to only rise a couple of degrees or so with highs in the lower to mid-40s. Tonight, cloudy skies with patchy fog overnight with showers likely in the evening and a chance of showers after midnight. Lows are expected in the lower 40s in Fort Kent and vicinity.

For Sunday, skies become mostly cloudy with a chance of scattered showers across the SJV. Highs warm into the middle 50s with shower activity finally tapering off Sunday evening as the low pressure in New Brunswick moves further east of the region. Mostly cloudy skies in the evening become partly cloudy overnight with temperatures dipping into the upper 30s/low 40s.

Memorial Day will feature partly cloudy to mostly sunny skies and temperatures in the upper 60s to around 70F in Fort Kent. Enough moisture and instability aloft will allow for isolated showers Monday afternoon with little precipitation expected. Monday night, isolated showers are possible with temperatures dropping into the the low 40s overnight.

4-7 Day Outlook: Tue. May 27 - Fri. May 30

Mostly sunny skies overspread the Valley as high pressure continues to build in on Tuesday, with mostly clear skies and dry conditions continuing Wednesday and Wednesday night. Highs reach the mid-70s Tuesday and upper 70s Wednesday with a low in the mid-to-upper 40s Tuesday night.

For the late week period, the National Weather Service in Caribou notes that "[h]igh pressure exits to the east early Thursday, with low pressure then approaching from the west late. Clouds will increase Thursday. Rain chances will then increase Thursday night. Low pressure will
begin to cross the region Friday with rain."

Longer range models indicate a chance of showers persists Saturday with partly cloudy skies developing Sunday into Monday as June gets underway.
