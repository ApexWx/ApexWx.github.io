The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Last Updated: October 23, 2023 at 9:10 am EDT

3-Day Outlook: Sun. Oct 22 - Tue. Oct 24

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

Water vapor satellite imagery shows the surface and upper-level low that has brought rain/showers to the region moving east in the Maritimes this morning. A cold front will be pulled into the Gulf of Maine tonight with rain coming to an end in Fort Kent and vicinity this evening. Gusty northwest winds 10-15 mph today will subside into the 5-10 mph range tonight with mostly cloudy skies across the Saint John Valley.

Mostly cloudy skies start the day Monday then become mostly clear by the evening with clear skies and calm winds Monday night allowing for the first hard freeze of the season with overnight temperatures looking to drop into the mid-to-upper 20s.

Mostly clear skies are expected Tuesday with southwest flow setting up at the surface as high pressure slides into the Atlantic off Cape Cod. Increasing clouds occur Tuesday night as a cold front in Quebec slides towards northern Maine.

Apex Wx Daily Summary

Today: Cloudy with a 70% chance for rain in the morning tapering off in the afternoon. Rainfall totals between 0.01 and 0.09-inch expected. High around 52. North wind 10-15 mph with gusts 20-25 mph possible.

Tonight: Mostly cloudy with a 10% chance for showers. Low near 36. North wind 6-8 mph.

Monday: Mostly cloudy in the morning then becoming partly cloudy to mostly clear in the afternoon. High around 50. North wind 5-10 mph becoming northwest in the afternoon.

Monday night: Mostly clear with a low near 28. West wind near calm.

Tuesday: Mostly sunny with a high near 55. Southwest wind 5-8 mph.

Tuesday night: Partly cloudy with a 30% chance for scattered showers. Low around 38. South wind 0-7 mph.

4-7 Day Outlook: Wed. Oct 25 - Sat. Oct 28

A weak area of low pressure tracking along the frontal boundary to our north will bring mostly cloudy skies and increasing chances for showers as the day progresses with showers likely Wednesday night before tapering off early Thursday. The front remains stalled to the north Thursday with partly sunny to partly cloudy skies. Chances for rain increase in the afternoon with scattered showers expected Thursday night into Friday.

Friday into Saturday morning looks to remain considerably cloudy with a chance for showers Friday and Friday night as moisture rides over the top of a high pressure ridge anchored over the Southeastern US under with the SJV is on the northern periphery of the high's influence. An area of low pressure tracks across the Great Lakes then to our north pulling a cold front across the region Saturday into Sunday. Showers decrease in frequency by Saturday afternoon.

For snow watchers (who have asked), while the long-range GFS and ECMWF computer models suggest the potential for some frozen precipitation around late October/start of November, there is too much uncertainty this far out to make any definite predication if any significant snowfall will occur, particularly given the above normal temperatures we have been experiencing for this time of year.
