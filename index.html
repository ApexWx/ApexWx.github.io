3-Day Outlook: Fri. May 5 - Sun. May 7

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

Happy Cinco de Mayo to those observing this holiday!

A few clouds are moving across the area this morning from the northeast and will be followed by a lower-level cumulus field as the morning progresses. High pressure building down from Hudson Bay will bring dry conditions to the Saint John Valley with the daytime high reaching the lower 60s. North winds are expected in the 8-10 mph with some gusts to around 17-18 mph. Tonight, partly cloudy skis in the evening will give way to mostly clear skies overnight as the aforementioned cumulus field dissipates. Tonight's low will be in the lower 40s with northwest winds around 5-6 mph.

For UMFK's Commencement Day 2023, mostly sunny skies will spread over Fort Kent thanks to high pressure that remains over the Hudson Bay. Fort Kent's high reaches the mid-60s with a northwest wind in the 9-13 mph range gusting up to 25 mph at times. Saturday night will feature mostly clear skies with a low in the lower 40s and northwest winds 4-6 mph.

Sunday, the large ridge of high pressure extending from the Hudson Bay to offshore of the Southeastern US will continue to bring fair, dry weather to the Valley. Sunday's high reaches the mid-60s with northwest winds mixing down in the 9-13 mph range with gusts to around 25 mph possible once more. Mostly sunny skies in the morning become partly cloudy in the afternoon as fair-weather cumulus forms thanks to some moisture at about 4K feet aloft.

Sunday night, partly cloudy skies are expected as a dry cold front drops down from the north. Overnight lows are expected to fall to around 40 with a light northwest wind.

While temperatures will warm nicely, area lakes and rivers are still very cold. Before venturing onto area waters, be sure to review Cold Water Safety. In particular, the National Weather Service notes that:
  • Body heat can be lost 4 times faster in cold water than in cold air.
  • Wearing a life jacket significantly increases chances of survival.
  • Cold shock can be just as severe and dangerous from water temperatures of 50-60F (10-15C) as it is from water at 35F (2C).
  • Gasping for a breath or rapid breathing from sudden immersion can be triggered by water as warm as 77F (25C).
4-7 Day Outlook: Mon. May 8 - Thu. May 11

High pressure over the Hudson Bay will slowly move southeast to the Mid-Atlantic coast by the end of the period. Cooler air behind Sunday night's cold front will produce highs in the mid-50s Monday and Tuesday with partly cloudy skies expected both days. A weak trough brings a chance for isolated rain showers Monday night, mixing with snow overnight into early Tuesday; however, no snow accumulation is anticipated from this system. Isolated showers wind down by Tuesday afternoon with mostly clear conditions overspreading the SJV once more. Monday night's low dips into the mid-30s. Wednesday and Thursday look mostly sunny with a high in the low 60s Wednesday and a low in the low 40s Wednesday night. Thursday's high reaches the mid-60s with an overnight low in the low 40s Thursday night.
