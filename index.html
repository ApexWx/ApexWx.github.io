3-Day Outlook: Saturday - Monday

2 inches of snow accumulated in Fort Kent yesterday with another 3 inches last night for a storm total of 5-inches as measured at 9 am this morning and reported to NWS Caribou.

Surface low pressure that brought yesterday's and last night's snowfall will continue to lift northeast away from the Valley. An upper-level trough trailing the system will move across the SJV today then east of the region tonight. Clouds will diminish as the day progresses with any lingering snow showers ending around noon. Skies become clear tonight with near calm winds and fresh snow allowing radiational cooling to take off and dropping overnight lows into the -15Â° to -25Â° for most locations east to west across northern Maine with some sheltered valleys in the North Woods possibly plunging to -30Â°.

High pressure will build in from the west Saturday with clearing skies in the afternoon and mostly clear skies overnight. Light wind and fresh snow will allow radiational cooling to drop temperatures into the -15Â° to -25Â° range east to west across the Crown Saturday night. I am looking at about -18Â° for Fort Kent and vicinity with temps possibly 2-3 degrees cooler depending on high clouds from the next system approaching and any light wind.

Clouds increase Sunday as a weak cold front approaches from the northwest bringing some scattered snow showers to the Valley Sunday night into Monday as it move across the area. Temperatures will be less frigid with daytime highs in the low 10s above and overnight lows dipping into the low single digits above in Fort Kent and vicinity.

Monday will see a chance for snow showers into the afternoon as the trough moves across the region followed by a chance for snow Monday night into Tuesday as energy off the Carolinas tracks towards Nova Scotia. Most models keep the low far enough east the only eastern portions of the Valley and the state will be affected; however, the Canadian model tracks the low a bit further west with higher snowfall for the SJV. While precise amounts are hard to determine this far out, this is probably a 2-3 inch storm system based on current model data.
