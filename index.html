4 to 7 Day Outlook: Friday - Monday

Secondary low pressure associated with a large system over the Great Lakes will track towards southern Maine Friday morning. Currently, enough cold air is expected to be near the surface in northern Maine that precipitation will change to snow early Friday with snow continuing through the morning before mixing with then changing to rain in the afternoon. With surface temperatures near freezing in the morning then rising into the low 40s as the day progresses, an inch or so of wet snow is all that is anticipated.

Precipitation changes to a rain/snow shower mix Friday night into Saturday morning with precipitation tapering off by the afternoon. The associated upper-level trough will move across the SJV Saturday into Sunday with a slight chance for rain showers Saturday night into Sunday morning.  Mostly cloudy skies Sunday give way to partly sunny skies Monday as high pressure builds in.

NWS Caribou notes in their hydrology discussion that, given the wintry mix then rain and rain showers later Friday, the "potential exists for a 0.5 to 1-inch of rain into near "ripe" or very "ripe" snowpack [meaning any rainfall will not be absorbed and will result
in water release from the snowpack]. This combined with temperatures this weekend in the mid-to-upper 40s will likely result in ice movement. As the ice rots, breaks up expect an increased potential of ice jams especially on the Aroostook, Allagash & St. John Rivers starting this weekend into next week."
