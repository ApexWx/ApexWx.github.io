The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & Vicinity by Apex Wx
Updated Tuesday, January 3 at 9:45 am EST

3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday
Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Conditions

A weak disturbance will move across the Valley today with plenty of cloudiness and a slight chance for snow showers. Mostly cloudy skies are expected tonight as a warm front lifts towards southern Maine. Weak low pressure will slide across the Gulf along the frontal boundary keeping plenty of clouds over the Valley.

A cold front moves south Wednesday with low-level moisture trapped beneath the cold layer allowing for some isolated snow showers across the region. High pressure building down from the north will push colder, drier air into the region Wednesday night with partly cloudy skies overspreading the SJV.

Moisture will be drawn into the region Thursday with snow overspreading Fort Kent and surrounding areas in the morning and continuing over the course of the day and into the overnight hours. NWS Caribou expects "generally 1-3 inches across the area with the most in the Central Highlands back into the Moosehead Region and North Woods. Lowest amounts across the St. John Valley and Downeast Islands."
