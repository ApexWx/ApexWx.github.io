Short-Range Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Surface low pressure will remain stationary over Maine today while the associated upper-level low moves east towards the region. Showers will be likely across the Valley later today with a slight chance for thunderstorms as some weak stability aloft creates some low-topped convection. Some graupel/ice pellets may mix in with showers thanks to low freezing levels aloft. Showers continue into the overnight hours before tapering off early Wednesday.

Wrap-around moisture and daytime heating keep a chance for showers across the SJV Wednesday as the low pressure area shifts east into the Maritimes. Partly cloudy skies develop Wednesday night as high pressure over the Upper Mississippi Valley builds east into the region. Party cloudy skies with a chance for afternoon showers are on tap Thursday with mostly clear skies Thursday night.

Note: For US Eastern Time on maps and charts, subtract 4 hours (Daylight Saving Time) or 5 hours (Standard Time) from UTC / Z* timestamp on the maps and charts. Visit timeanddate.com for more time zone conversion information and additional time-related data.

*UTC / Z / GMT = Coordinated Universal Time / Zulu Time / Greenwich Mean Time -- Time is based on 0° longitude running through the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, UK.
