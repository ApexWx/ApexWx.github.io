Current NWS Caribou, ME Hazardous Weather Outlook

3-Day Outlook: Sat. June 22 - Mon. June 24

Saturday features mostly partly cloudy to partly sunny skies with a slight chance of afternoon showers for Fort Kent and vicinity thanks to a frontal boundary stalled out near the Downeast coastline. Highs reach the low 80s. Skies become mostly cloudy with increasing chance of showers are possible Saturday night as low pressure and associated fronts approach from the Great Lakes region. Lows are expected to be in the upper 50s.

A warm front ahead of the Great Lakes low brings a chance of showers Sunday morning with a steadier rain expected Sunday afternoon into early Monday morning. Highs reach the low 70s Sunday. Overnight lows dip into the mid-50s Sunday night.

 A chance of showers are expected from early Monday along with some patchy fog across the Valley. Highs in the mid-70s with partly sunny to mostly cloudy skies are expected. Monday night, a chance of showers with lows in the middle 50s are anticipated.

Daily Summary

Today: Partly sunny, with a high near 82. Calm wind becoming southwest around 6 mph in the afternoon.

Tonight: Mostly cloudy, with a low around 58. Calm wind.

Sunday: Scattered showers before noon, then rain, mainly after noon. The rain could be heavy at times. High near 70. East wind 6 to 10 mph, with gusts as high as 20 mph. Chance of precipitation is 90%. New precipitation amounts between a half and three quarters of an inch possible.

Sunday Night: Rain. Patchy fog after 2am. Low around 55. Northeast wind around 8 mph. Chance of precipitation is 80%. New precipitation amounts between a quarter and half of an inch possible.

Monday: A chance of showers. Patchy fog before 7am. Otherwise, partly sunny, with a high near 75. Northeast wind around 9 mph. Chance of precipitation is 50%. New precipitation amounts between a tenth and quarter of an inch possible.

Monday Night: A chance of showers, mainly before 5am. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 55. Light and variable wind becoming northwest around 6 mph after midnight. Chance of precipitation is 30%.


4-7 Day Outlook: Tue. June 25 - Thu. June 28

Tuesday looks to become mostly sunny by the afternoon as high pressure builds in with a chance of morning showers. Highs reach the mid-80s. Tuesday night, mostly clear skies are expected with lows in the low 60s.

Clouds increase as a cold front approaches from the west Wednesday with a chance of scattered showers and isolated thunderstorms by the afternoon. Highs are expected in the mid-80s. Wednesday night, the cold front moves through the region Wednesday night with showers likely along with a chance of thunderstorms.. Lows fall into the upper 50s overnight.

Scattered showers with isolated afternoon thunderstorms are possible Thursday with highs in the mid-70s. Skies become partly cloudy Thursday night with some scattered showers and isolated thunderstorms possible. Lows dip into the upper 40s.

High pressure builds in from the west with mostly sunny skies and highs in the mid-70s Friday. Partly cloudy skies with lows in the lower 50s are expected in Fort Kent Friday night.

June 22: Record High Temperatures Likely Across the Mid-Atlantic; Severe Weather in the Upper Midwest and Southwest
