Short-Term Outlook: Thursday -  Saturday

Canadian igh pressure continues building down from James Bay in Canada today with mostly sunny skies and cooler conditions. Breezy north winds thanks to the pressure gradient between the departing low pressure system and incoming ridge of high pressure. Fire weather conditions are moderate across northern Maine today due to low relative humidity values and breezy north/northwest winds. Therefore, any permitted burning should be carefully considered to avoid the potential for wildfire spread. Highs top out in the low 60s across the SJV today. Winds diminish as the day progresses night with temperatures falling into the lower 40s across the Valley tonight.

Weak low pressure will drop down from the northwest Friday with a slight chance for afternoon showers--primarily from the Clayton Lake region south/southeastwards, though a stray shower in Fort Kent, Frenchville, or the Allagash cannot be ruled out. This system will be a quick mover with high pressure ridging back in Friday night with mostly clear skies across northern Maine. Daytime highs in the upper 60s to low 70s across the SJV Friday with temperatures in the lower 40s across northern Maine once again Friday night.

Saturday will feature plenty of sunshine and warmer temperatures with daytime highs topping out in the middle 70s across most of the region and overnight lows in the middle 40s.

Medium-Range Outlook: Sunday - Wednesday
 
Sunday begins sunny with clouds increasing in the afternoon as a deep upper-level trough of low pressure moves into the region bringing increasing cloudiness and showers to the Valley Mondaythrough Wednesday as the system slowly moves east through the midweek period.

The upper-level pattern features a large ridge over the western US and a deep trough over the eastern US which could prove persistent through much of the upcoming week with unsettled conditions and periodic showers throughout the period.
