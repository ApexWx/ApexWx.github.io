3-Day Outlook: Thursday - Saturday

Winter Weather Advisory in effect until 6 am Friday: Snow expected. Total snow accumulations of 4 to 6 inches expected with locally over 9 inches in higher elevations. Winds gusting as high as 35 mph producing patchy blowing snow Thursday into Thursday night. Travel could be very difficult. Patchy blowing snow could significantly reduce visibility and cause  localized hazardous travel. The hazardous conditions could impact the morning and evening commute. Power outages are possible.

Surface low pressure and an associated upper-level low stalled near the Bay of Fundy will move east today then track south of Nova Scotia Friday. The system will gradually weaken as it moves into the Atlantic Saturday. Cold northerly air is being channeled into the Valley today on gusty north winds. Snow is expected throughout the day with much of northern Maine seeing 4-6 inches of the white stuff. 2-3 inches are expected along the Maine/New Brunswick border. Portions of the North Woods will have6-8 inches accumulation with the Katahdin region having accumulations in the 8-12 inch range.

Snow Friday morning changes to rain through the afternoon then tapers to rain and snow showers Friday evening. Snow showers persist overnight into Saturday morning before changing to rain shower in the afternoon. Evening rain showers Saturday mix with snow overnight into early Sunday before ending as the Atlantic low finally moves away and high pressure begins to build into the SJV. Windy conditions today will remain breezy Friday and Saturday with areas of blowing snow possible tonight.
