Winter weather advisory in effect until 4 pm this afternoon: Mixed precipitation expected. Total snow accumulations of 2 to 4 inches, sleet accumulations of around one-tenth of an inch and ice accumulations of up to one tenth of an inch expected. Plan on slippery road conditions.

Overrunning snow associated with a warm front lifting across the region will continue into the midday period before changing to freezing rain then to all rain in the afternoon warmer air arrives aloft. Rain continues into the evening hours as low pressure moves across the region. Colder air moves back in behind the low with rain/freezing rain changing to all snow then tapering to snow showers and ending after midnight. Around 3-4 inches of snowfall are expected for most northern Maine locations.

Mostly cloudy skies overnight become increasingly cloudy Monday as another area of low pressure approaches from the southwest and tracks towards the Downeast coast. Snow overspreads the region Monday afternoon with snow likely in the evening before tapering to snow showers after midnight as this system also moves east.

Tuesday will see decreasing clouds and chances for precipitation with brisk northwest winds and cold conditions. Tuesday night is expected to be mostly clear with near seasonal lows in the lower single digits.
