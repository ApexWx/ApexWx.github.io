7-Day Outlook: Thu. July 10 - Wed. July 16
Updated, July 10 at 9:10 am EDT

Thursday -  Saturday
An upper-level trough to the west will approach today and cross the Valley Friday. High pressure will then build in later Friday into Saturday. Currently, some timing differences on various forecast models are noted, but showers and isolated thunderstorms are possible. Precipitable water values (how much rain would fall if all the moisture in the atmosphere over the region condensed) are about 1-inch so heavy downpours are possible with any storms. Additionally, gusty storm winds are possible, too. The Storm Prediction Center has placed the Allagash and North Woods at marginal risk for isolated strong/severe storms today. Highs look to reach the low 80s today. East/southeast winds 0-5 mph are expected.

Tonight, mostly cloudy to overcast skies with a chance of showers in the evening then showers likely with isolated thunderstorms after midnight. Lows fall into the upper 50s to near 60F overnight. Light southeast winds are anticipated.

Showers are likely Friday as the trough crosses the region with scattered afternoon thunderstorms including isolated strong/severe storms for northern Maine with potentially damaging thunderstorm wind gusts being the main threat. Highs look to top out in the middle 70s with southeast winds 0-5 mph. Tomorrow night, expect skies to become partly cloudy with scattered showers in the evening and patchy fog overnight. Lows fall into the upper 50s with near calm winds.

High pressure builds in Saturday with mostly sunny skies and highs ranging from the upper 70s to low 80s. Mostly clear skies Saturday night with lows in the upper 50s to near 60F. Light south winds are expected to develop over the course of the day into the overnight hours.

Sunday - Wednesday
Partly cloudy to partly sunny skies are expected Sunday as high pressure moves southeast of the region. An upper-level disturbance approaches from the west later in the day producing a slight chance of showers Sunday night with increasing cloudiness across the SJV as moisture moves in aloft in the return flow around the departing high pressure ridge. Highs reach the low 80s with overnight lows in the lower 60s.

Monday, partly cloudy to partly sunny skies with a chance of mainly afternoon showers and thunderstorms and breezy southerly winds as a weakening cold front approaches. Highs top out in the low 80s. Mostly cloudy skies overspread the Fort Kent region Monday night with a chance of showers and isolated thunderstorms overnight. Lows fall into the lower 60s.

The dissipating cold front moves east of the region Tuesday producing partly sunny skies with lingering showers and isolated afternoon thunderstorms possible. Highs in the low 80s and lows in the lower 60s can be anticipated.

For Wednesday, mostly sunny skies overspread the Valley was high pressure builds across the area with a stalled frontal boundary to the northwest. This boundary will provide a slight chance of isolated afternoon showers and thunderstorms in Fort Kent and vicinity. Highs in the low-to-mid-80s are expected. Wednesday night, a slight chance of showers remains with temperatures falling into the lower 60s under partly cloudy skies.
