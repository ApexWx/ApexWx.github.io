The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine by Apex Wx
Updated: May 5, 2024 at 7:20 am EDT

3-Day Outlook: Sun. May 5 - Tue. May 7

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information.

High pressure will slide southeast of Nova Scotia today while a cold front approaches from the west. The front will cross Maine tonight into Monday with rain likely this evening into the overnight hours. Rain tapers to scattered showers Monday morning that taper off as the day progresses. Skies become partly cloudy Monday night with partly sunny skies across the Valley Tuesday along with gusty northwest winds. Canadian high pressure continues to spread across the SJV Tuesday night with chilly overnight lows under partly cloudy skies falling into the lower-to-middle 30s in Fort Kent and vicinity.

Daily Summary

Today: Scattered showers between 3pm and 5pm, then rain likely after 5pm. Increasing clouds, with a high near 63. South wind 10 to 14 mph, with gusts as high as 25 mph. Chance of precipitation is 60%. New precipitation amounts of less than a tenth of an inch possible.

Tonight: Rain. Low around 46. South wind 8 to 10 mph, with gusts as high as 20 mph. Chance of precipitation is 90%. New precipitation amounts between a tenth and quarter of an inch possible.

Monday: Scattered showers, mainly before 2pm. Mostly cloudy, with a high near 69. South wind 7 to 9 mph becoming west in the afternoon. Chance of precipitation is 40%.

Monday Night: Partly cloudy, with a low around 41. Northwest wind around 8 mph.

Tuesday: Partly sunny, with a high near 60. Northwest wind 11 to 14 mph, with gusts as high as 25 mph.

Tuesday Night: Partly cloudy, with a low around 34. Northwest wind 6 to 10 mph, with gusts as high as 20 mph.

4-7 Day Outlook: Wed. May 8 - Sat. May 11

Mostly sunny skies Wednesday with increasing cloudiness Wednesday evening as a low pressure system approaches from the Midwest that is expected to produces some isolated showers overnight into early Thursday. Mostly sunny skies overspread the SJV Thursday with increased cloudiness in the evening and a chance for scattered showers overnight into Friday. The occluding low pressure system will approach Friday into Saturday with a continued chance for scattered showers with precipitation models indicating one-tenth to one-quarter inch north and one-quarter to one-half inch rainfall possible in southern Maine (though precipitation totals may still change since this is seven days out on forecast models runs).

Daily Summary

Wednesday: Partly sunny, with a high near 60. Northeast wind around 8 mph.

Wednesday Night: A slight chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 36. Chance of precipitation is 20%.

Thursday: Mostly sunny, with a high near 59.

Thursday Night: A chance of showers, mainly after midnight. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 39. Chance of precipitation is 30%.

Friday: A chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a high near 57. Chance of precipitation is 30%.

Friday Night: A chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a low around 40. Chance of precipitation is 30%.

Saturday: A chance of showers. Mostly cloudy, with a high near 57. Chance of precipitation is 40%.

Saturday Night: Overcast with rain showers at times. Low 37. Chance of rain 50%.
