3-Day Outlook: Wed. June 26 - Fri. June 28

Low pressure in central Canada tracking east into Qu√©bec will lift a warm front across the region today with a slight chance of showers in the morning, then afternoon showers are likely along with scattered thunderstorms for the Saint John Valley. The cold front moves across the region tonight with rain likely tonight into Thursday morning. Highs reach the low 80s with temperatures in the middle 50s overnight.

The cold front exits the Valley Thursday with morning with rain tapering to showers that continue into the afternoon. Enough instability aloft also allows for a slight chance of afternoon/evening thunderstorms Thursday. Skies become partly sunny Thursday afternoon with highs in the upper 60s. Thursday night, partly cloudy skies overspread the region with a chance of showers overnight ending early Friday. Lows dip into the mid-40s.

Friday, high pressure spreads across region with mostly sunny skies. Partly cloudy skies spread across the SJV Friday night into early Saturday. Highs reach the lower 70s with overnight temperatures in the low 50s.

 4-7 Day Outlook: Sat. June 29 - Tue. July 2

High pressure slides east into the Atlantic Saturday while a cold front approaches from the west with skies becoming partly sunny to mostly cloudy with a chance of showers developing in the afternoon. Showers are likely by Saturday evening as the front approaches with plenty of moisture aloft, and showers are likely Saturday night into Sunday. NWS Caribou notes that "models are in decent agreement on a heavy rain event Saturday night...Fortunately, this will be another progressive event with rain moving out of the area [fairly quickly]." Highs Saturday reach the low -70s with overnight lows in the mid-50s.

Showers likely Sunday with scattered thunderstorms possible in the afternoon. Showers and thunderstorms wind down in the evening with isolated showers lingering overnight behind the front. Highs in the upper 70s are followed by temperatures in the lower 50s Sunday night.

High pressure is currently expected to build into the Valley Monday with mostly sunny skies and highs climbing into the mid-70s. Monday night, mostly clear skies are anticipated with temperatures falling into the lower 50s.

High pressure remains across the Valley Tuesday with sunny skies and highs in the low 80s. Partly cloudy skies are expected Tuesday night with lows in the mid-50s.
