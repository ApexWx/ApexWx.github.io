3-Day Outlook: Fri. June 30 - Sun. July 2

Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook
Click for the latest information

The upper-level trough that brought heavy downpours to the area yesterday will gradually move east today. A stalled surface boundary will remain roughly west of Highway 11 today with mostly cloudy skies this morning becoming partly sunny in the afternoon. A chance for showers persists along with a chance for isolated afternoon thunderstorms. Rainfall totals in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range are possible with higher totals amounts possible in any storms. Highs climb into the upper 70s. Tonight, partly cloudy skies continue through midnight with increasing clouds after as the next upper-level low/trough approaches from the west. Some scattered evening showers are possible with patchy fog overnight. Lows fall into the lower 60s.

Saturday, mostly cloudy skies return with a chance for showers and isolated afternoon thunderstorms. Rainfall totals will be in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range with higher amounts possible in storms. Saturday's high reaches the upper 70s. Saturday night, showers are likely with patchy fog overnight. Temperatures drop into the lower 60s under overcast skies. Rainfall amounts look to be in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range overnight.

The upper-level trough remains to the west in Quebec Sunday with disturbances circulating around it over the Saint John Valley. Showers are likely Sunday morning with a chance for showers and isolated afternoon thunderstorms. Precipitation amounts in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range are expected once again with higher totals possible in storms. Daytime highs reach the middle 70s. Sunday night, mostly cloudy skies with showers likely and a low in the lower 60s can be expected. Rainfall amounts in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range are anticipated.

4-7 Day Outlook: Mon. July 3 - Thu. July 6

The upper-level trough remains stalled to the west until around midweek when it will finally move east with a weak ridge of high pressure building in by Wednesday/Thursday. High moisture content in the air mass will allow any sunshine to trigger showers in the afternoon as heat contributes to atmospheric destabilization. So, a chance for showers Monday into Independence Day with showers likely Tuesday afternoon along with isolated thunder. Wednesday will feature partly sunny skies; however, a chance for showers continues. Partly sunny skies with a chance for afternoon showers can be expected for Thursday based on current forecast model indications.
