Short-Term Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Surface high pressure centered over northern Pennsylvania/southern New York State this morning will bring partly cloudy skies to the SJV today. Clouds will increase this afternoon ahead of a weak cold front dropping in from the northwest with some scattered showers possible later this afternoon.

A slight chance for showers continues into the evening hours. Partly cloudy skies are expected overnight and Wednesday as the frontal boundary stalls out over the region and weakens further. An isolated shower is possible Wednesday.

An off-shore front with subtropical moisture will back towards New  England Thursday with an area of low pressure running along it. This system will bring a chance for at least scattered shower activity to the Valley Thursday with precipitation dependent on how close the low tracks to the Downeast coast.

Medium-Range Outlook: Friday - Monday

High pressure brings partly cloudy skies Thursday with a chance for isolated morning showers thanks to a weak trough aloft. Another trough to the southeast brings a chance for scattered showers Friday afternoon through Saturday afternoon with a chance for isolated thunderstorms Saturday afternoon and evening. Partly cloudy skies with a chance for isolated to scattered showers are expected Sunday with warming temperatures and increasing humidity.
