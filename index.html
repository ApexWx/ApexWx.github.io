At 4:06 P.M. EDT on July 6, 2023, Earth will reach the point in its orbit when it is farthest from the sun. It will then begin to move closer until it reaches perihelion on January 2, 2024.

A ridge of high pressure will build across the region today with sunny, hot, and humid conditions for Fort Kent and the Saint John Valley of Maine. Daytime highs climb into the low 90s with heat indices in the middle 90s for most of the County. Atmospheric soundings show enough instability aloft to trigger isolated afternoon showers and thunderstorms that may continue into the evening hours before waning overnight with the loss of daytime heating. Tonight, partly cloudy skies with some isolated evening showers and thunderstorms with a low in the middle 60s can be expected. Some patchy fog is possible overnight. Light southwest winds today with light south winds are expected tonight.

High pressure will move east Friday with partly sunny skies in the morning become mostly cloudy in the afternoon as a cold front approaches from the west. Showers are likely in the afternoon along with scattered thunderstorms. Rainfall totals in the 0.25 to 0.50-inch range with higher amounts possible in any storms, which may feature heavy downpours. Mostly cloudy skies with a chance for showers and evening thunderstorms are expected Friday night with lows dipping into the mid-60s once more.

The cold front is expected to stall out near the western Maine/Quebec border Saturday with hot, humid conditions for the Saint John Valley. A chance for showers in the morning with showers likely in the afternoon along with scattered thunderstorms with rainfall totals in the 0.10 to 0.25-inch range. Locally higher totals will be possible in any storms that occur. Saturday's high peaks in the middle 80s with west/southwest flow across the region. Saturday night, partly cloudy skies with a chance for showers and isolated thunderstorms continuing into early Sunday. Lows dip into the mid-60s with light southeast winds.

For the upcoming weekend, NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook notes that "[s]howers and thunderstorms with heavy rain and possible gusty winds are possible late Friday afternoon into Friday evening, especially in northwest portions of the region. Other showers and thunderstorms with locally heavy rainfall may also occur over portions of the region every afternoon and evening from Saturday through Monday."

4-7 Day Outlook: Sun. July 9 - Wed. July 12

The period remains showery as upper-level troughs and an upper-level low in Canada affect the region's weather next week. Additionally, models indicate an area of low pressure moving up into the Northeast from the Mid-Atlantic, which could bring more showers to the region early next week.

So, Sunday looks mostly cloudy with showers, especially in the afternoon and evening with highs in the middle 80s and lows in the mid-60s. Monday will be mostly cloudy with showers likely in the afternoon along with scattered thunderstorms. Highs reach the upper 70s with a low in the mid-60s. Tuesday features mostly cloudy skies with showers likely. Highs in the upper 70s with lows in the lower 60s can be expected. Wednesday becomes partly sunny with a chance for showers, especially in the afternoon. Wednesday's high tops out in the lower 80s with an overnight low in the lower 60s.
