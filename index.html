The Week Ahead from Apex Wx
Updated Friday, December 16 at 9:40 am EST

Friday - Sunday Dec. 16-18, 2022

NWS Caribou has issued a Winter Storm Watch from Saturday morning through Sunday evening: Heavy snow possible. Total snow accumulations in excess of 8 inches possible.Travel could be very difficult. Gusty winds could bring down tree branches.

Today will be cloudy, cold, with a slight chance for snow showers in Fort Kent and vicinity. Then, a significant snowstorm is expected to impact the region through the weekend.

Low pressure near the Mid-Atlantic coast will track into the Gulf of Maine tonight into early Saturday and slowly cross the to the Bay of Fundy by Sunday morning. The low will then track into New Brunswick Sunday into Monday before lifting east away from the region.

Snow is expected to overspread the Valley early Saturday with snow continuing through early Monday morning. NWS Caribou notes that the storm will be a "multi-phased, long duration snowfall [that] will impact much of northern and eastern Maine this weekend. While snowfall rates will generally be modest, snow banding is forecast to produce locally higher rates and difficult travel conditions at times. The snow will initially spread across Central and Downeast areas, then progress northward through the day Saturday. Snow will linger across northern areas through Sunday night, becoming increasingly fluffy with higher snow to liquid ratios leading to potential for greater snowfall rates. Multi-day accumulations in excess of 10 inches are possible across the watch area and portions of the warning area."

Monday - Thursday, December 19-22, 2022

On Monday, the upper-level low associated with this weekend's system will move east of the region with lingering snow showers expected over the course of the day into the overnight period for Fort Kent and vicinity. Snow showers taper off after midnight with an upper-ridge building across the SJV. Mostly cloudy skies will remain in place Tuesday and Tuesday night.

An upper-trough may clip northern Maine Wednesday with a chance for some snow shower under partly sunny skies. Thursday remains partly cloudy as weak high pressure builds across the area. Clouds increase as a strengthening low pressure area to the southwest begins to approach with a slight chance for afternoon snow/snow showers.

Models are currently inconsistent with a possible significant snow storm towards Christmas, so this will need to be monitored for a potential holiday storm for the region.

The Winter Solstice for the Northern Hemisphere occurs at 4:48 pm EST Wednesday, December 21.
