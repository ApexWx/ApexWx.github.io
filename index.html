7-Day Outlook: Thu. Nov. 13 - Wed. Nov. 19
Updated November 13 at 7:30 am EST by Apex Wx

‚ùÑÔ∏è Low pressure lifting across the area may bring a period of significant snow over the north Sunday into Sunday evening.‚ùÑÔ∏è

Thursday - Saturday
An upper level low will keep unsettled and mostly cloudy conditions over Fort Kent and vicinity today with a slight chance of snow showers producing less than 0.5-inch of snowfall, if any. The upper low moves northeast away from the region tonight while a flattening ridge of high pressure builds into the region producing partly cloudy/partly sunny skies Friday and partly cloudy skies Friday night. Skies become mostly sunny Saturday morning with clouds increasing Saturday night as a low pressure system approaches with increasing chances of snow for the Valley Saturday night. Highs in the 30s with lows in the 20s for the period.

Sunday - Wednesday
A storm system will move through Maine on Sunday as upper-level energy dives southeast and a new coastal low forms. This system will bring mainly rain to Bangor and Downeast areas, while northern Maine sees mostly snow. As the storm moves into the Maritimes Sunday night, colder air will spread south, changing rain to snow across most of the region.

The greatest chance for accumulating snow is in northern areas Sunday night, while precipitation should taper off earlier farther south. Forecast models differ on how much snow will fall, with some suggesting heavier totals in the north, so upcoming forecasts bear monitoring.

On Monday, the storm moves away, leaving lingering snow showers across the Valley and strong northwest winds gusting up to 40 mph. Drier, cooler air settles in for Tuesday and Wednesday, bringing mostly cloudy to partly sunny skies and below-normal temperatures for mid-November. Highs in the mid-to-upper 30s with overnight lows in the mid-20s expected.

Thunderstorm/Severe Weather Outlook
A slow-moving upper-level low will approach the West Coast today, spreading widespread clouds and moisture across California. While winds aloft will be strong and shifting with height, the atmosphere near the surface will remain too stable for thunderstorms.
