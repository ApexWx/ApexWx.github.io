7-Day Outlook: Sat. June 14 - Fri. June 20
Updated: Saturday, June 14, 2025 at 9:31 am EDT

Saturday - Monday
Mostly sunny skies continue to overspread the SJV today as high pressure remains across the region. With light winds and abundant sunshine highs reach the upper 60s with mostly clear skies across the region with an area of low pressure tracking well south of the area off the Mid-Atlantic. Overnight temperatures dip into the low 40s under mostly clear to partly cloudy skies.

Mostly sunny skies are expected Sunday though a weak upper air disturbance in the flow aloft crossing the region around midday will bring patchy clouds and some isolated showers into early afternoon. Otherwise, mostly clear skies are on tap Sunday night. Daytime highs in the low 70s with overnight lows in the middle 40s are expected.

For Monday, expect mostly sunny to partly cloudy skies with highs in the mid-to-upper 70s in Fort Kent and southwest flow across the area. Monday night, skies become partly cloudy as a cold front trailing from a low in Qu√©bec approaches. A slight chance of showers is possible after midnight. Lows fall into the lower 50s.

Tuesday - Friday
Unsettled weather is looking probable for this period. Low pressure tracks across Qu√©bec into Labrador Tuesday with a cold stalling west of the region. Humidity will increase on Wednesday with dew points rising into the mid-50s to low 60s as high pressure moves out over the Atlantic. Low pressure tracking across Qu√©bec into Labrador could combine with the increasing moisture to bring some showers and isolated afternoon thunderstorms, especially in central and northern areas.

Another system will crosses the Great Lakes and approaches from southern Canada on Thursday, bringing another warm and humid day with the chance for more showers and thunderstorms, again mostly in central and northern parts of the state.

Showers and isolated thunderstorms may continue Thursday night into Friday as this system passes to the north. By Friday afternoon and evening, the system will move into the northern Maritimes, with some lingering showers for the SJV. Drier air is expected to move in later Friday, depending on how fast the system clears out. Looking ahead, partly cloudy to mostly sunny skies look possible next weekend with dry conditions for Fort Kent and surrounding communities. Highs in the low 70s Sat. and mid-70s Sun. with overnight lows in the 50s, based on longer-range forecast models.
