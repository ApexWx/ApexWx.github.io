What is UTC Time?
UTC (Coordinated Universal Time), also called Zulu (Z) Time, is the standard time reference used by meteorologists, weather models, satellites, aviation, and NOAA products worldwide. During Daylight Saving Time, Eastern Daylight Time (EDT) is 4 hours behind UTC. During Standard Time, Eastern Standard Time (EST) is 5 hours behind UTC. See timeanddate.com for more time zone information.

7-Day Outlook: Thu. July 23 – Wed. July 29, 2026
Updated July 23 at 9:50 AM EDT

Thu. July 23 – Sat. July 25
Surface high pressure centered south of the Great Lakes builds into the Valley today into Friday with mostly sunny skies overspreading Fort Kent and vicinity. An upper trough following yesterday's frontal system will bring a chance of scattered showers to the Valley this afternoon. Highs top out in the upper 60s to near 70°F with west wind 10-15 mph with gusts 25-30 mph possible. Tonight looks mostly clear as high pressure remains in control. Lows fall into the upper 40s to near 50°F with south-southwest winds 0-5 mph.

High pressure remains in control Friday as spreads from the Ohio Valley to the Mid-Atlantic. Mostly sunny skies with temperatures in the low 80s and southwest wind 8-10 mph. Friday night, mostly clear with lows in the lower 50s and near calm winds in Fort Kent and vicinity. Winds become south 0+-5 mph.

High pressure remains in control of the region's weather Saturday with mostly sunny skies and warmer conditions with highs reaching the middle 80s. Saturday night looks partly cloudy and mild with overnight lows in the upper 50s to near 60°F. South wind 0-5 mph expected.

Sun. July 26 – Wed. July 29
High pressure will continue to dominate the Valley's weather Saturday and Sunday. Mostly sunny skies continue to overspread Fort Kent and vicinity Saturday with highs climbing into the mid-80s. South wind 0-5 mph expected. Saturday night, high pressure drifts east into the Maritimes with partly cloudy skies and lows in the upper 50s to around 60°F. South wind 0-5 mph expected.

For Sunday, mostly sunny skies continue across the SJV. Highs reach the mid-80s for most locations with south wind in the 5-10 mph range. A cold front approaching from Quebec may trigger some isolated afternoon showers and thunderstorms with a 20% chance of precipitation. Sunday night, partly cloudy skies are expected with lows in the upper 50s to near 60°F.  Southeast winds fall to near calm overnight.

The cold front continues to slide across Quebec Monday with mostly sunny skies and some scattered afternoon showers and thunderstorms possible with a 30% chance of precipitation. The high peaks in the lower 80s with light south winds across the area. Monday night, partly cloudy skies with a low in the upper 50s and light southeast winds expected along with some isolated showers possible overnight.

Low pressure looks to approach from the southwest Tuesday bringing more widespread shower activity in conjunction with the approaching cold front. Tuesday's high reaches around 80°F in Fort Kent with southeast winds in the 5-10 mph range. A 40% chance of mainly afternoon showers/thunderstorms can be expected. Tuesday night, partly cloudy skies with a 50% chance of showers and lows in the mid-to-upper 50s. Southeast wind 0-5 mph across the SJV.

For Wednesday, low pressure looks to track along the Maine coast with a 50% chance continued chance of showers and thunderstorms for the Saint John Valley. High in the mid-to-upper 70s with light southeast wind. Wednesday night, a chance of scattered showers and thunderstorms continues with lows in the mid-to-upper 50s. Winds shift to the northwest overnight in the 0-5 mph range.

Outlooks are typically updated form 7-9 AM weekdays and 8-10 AM weekends and holidays.

Tropical Storm Bertha, Heavy Rain, and Dangerous Heat Create Widespread Weather Threats
Tropical Storm Bertha is bringing heavy rain, tropical storm conditions, and a risk of isolated flash flooding and coastal flooding along parts of the Gulf Coast, especially near Louisiana and southern Texas. Heavy rainfall is also expected across the Southeast, the Carolinas, the Tennessee Valley, and parts of the southern Mid-Atlantic through Friday, increasing the potential for flash flooding. Monsoonal moisture will continue to produce showers and thunderstorms across the Southwest and Great Basin, with localized flooding possible. Severe thunderstorms may also develop across portions of the Plains and northern tier. Meanwhile, dangerous heat will persist across the Southern Tier and expand into the Great Basin and Northern Plains, with temperatures reaching the mid-90s to above 100 degrees and heat indices exceeding 110 degrees in some areas.

Severe Thunderstorm Risk Targets Western Kansas and Several Other Regions Today
Severe thunderstorms are possible this afternoon and evening across parts of the central and southern High Plains, with western Kansas facing the greatest threat. Storms may produce large hail and damaging wind gusts, especially if they become more organized during the evening. Additional isolated severe storms are possible from the middle Texas coast into Deep South Texas as Tropical Storm Bertha increases moisture and instability, raising the risk of damaging downburst winds. Isolated severe thunderstorms may also develop across the Columbia Basin and northern Intermountain West, bringing locally damaging winds and small to marginally severe hail. Along the eastern Carolinas, a hot and humid air mass could support strong thunderstorms capable of producing intense wind gusts through sunset.

🌧️Excessive Rainfall Outlook 🌧️
Learn more about Excessive Rainfall Risk

Persistent Heavy Rain Raises Significant Flash Flood Threat Across the Southeast
A moderate risk of excessive rainfall continues across central and northeastern North Carolina into far southeastern Virginia, where repeated rounds of heavy rain may cause numerous flash floods through tonight. Moisture from the Gulf of Mexico and Tropical Storm Bertha is feeding slow-moving thunderstorms along a stalled front, increasing flooding concerns in urban areas, including Virginia Beach, Norfolk, Chesapeake, and Portsmouth. A slight risk of flash flooding also extends across parts of the Carolinas, Georgia, Alabama, and Mississippi, where repeated storms may overwhelm drainage systems. Additional flash flood risks are expected tonight across portions of Nebraska, Kansas, and eastern Colorado. In South Texas, Tropical Storm Bertha may bring beneficial rainfall with isolated flooding, while scattered monsoon storms could produce localized flooding across parts of Colorado, Wyoming, and Utah.

🔥Fire Weather Outlook🔥
Learn more about Fire Weather Outlooks

Dry Thunderstorms and Gusty Winds Increase Wildfire Danger Across the Northwest
Dry and windy weather will increase the risk of new wildfires across parts of the Pacific Northwest and Northern Rockies today. Isolated thunderstorms are expected from the Columbia Basin into the Northern Rockies, but many storms may produce little rainfall while still generating lightning capable of igniting dry vegetation. Recent hot and dry conditions have left grasses and forests highly susceptible to fire, and gusty winds will make any new or existing fires spread more quickly. The greatest concern is where dry thunderstorms overlap with low humidity and strong winds. Meanwhile, parts of the Southern Plains, especially near the Oklahoma and Texas Panhandles, will also experience elevated fire weather conditions. Dry air, warm temperatures, gusty winds, and cured grasses will create favorable conditions for wildfire starts and spread.

How accurate are forecasts? On average, for the first 72 hours the accuracy is 90 percent. For days 4-7 it is 70 percent. After seven days, it is 50 percent—just good for indicating trends. On social media and other similar sites, posts that show a forecast model run a week out are just for clicks and likes, and are not considered reliable. Extended forecasts are subject to change as weather patterns evolve so should be considered guidance rather than precise predictions. Check for updated forecasts and advisories as conditions develop.
