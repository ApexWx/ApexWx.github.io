Low pressure over the Maritimes will slide into the Atlantic today as a large ridge of high pressure slides into the eastern US. The upper-level low moving across Maine this morning will slide into the Gulf later today then move east tonight. Cloudy skies with isolated to scattered showers will occur across the Saint John Valley again today into this evening. Clouds then diminish tonight as the lows move away and high pressure pushes into the region.

Skies become partly cloudy with some patchy fog possible overnight as winds fall to near calm. Partly sunny skies Wednesday morning give way to mostly clear skies in the evening as high pressure moves to the southeast of Maine. Mostly clear skies remain across the region Wednesday night into Thursday morning.
