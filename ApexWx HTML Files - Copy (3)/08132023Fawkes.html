Lancaster, PA, USA — Sun and Moon Times

3-Day Outlook: Sun. Aug 13 - Tue. Aug 15

NWS State College Hazardous Weather Conditions
Click for the latest information.

Mostly cloudy skies develop Monday afternoon with a chance for showers and scattered thunderstorms as a warm front today followed by a cold front crossing the region tonight. Showers and scattered thunderstorms are expected Monday night into Tuesday morning with scattered strong-to-severe storms possible. NOAA's Weather Prediction Center has southern Pennsylvania, including Lancaster and vicinity, at a slight risk for excessive rainfall. Currently, rainfall totals less than 0.10-inch today and between 0.50 and 0.75-inch tonight.

An upper-level trough crosses the region Tuesday with partly sunny skies and a chance for scattered showers and isolated thunderstorms into the afternoon. Tuesday night, high pressure builds in with mostly clear skies overnight.

Wednesday, high pressure builds across the Lower Susquehanna Valley with mostly sunny skies. High pressure will build across the area Wednesday night with mostly clear skies.

4-7 Day Outlook: Thu. Aug 17 - Sun. Aug 20

High pressure brings mostly sunny skies Thursday with mostly clear to partly cloudy skies Thursday night. An approaching cold front brings a chance for isolated showers and thunderstorms to the Lancaster region after midnight. Friday, partly cloudy to mostly sunny skies are expected with a slight chance for showers and isolated afternoon thunder. High pressure then builds in for the remainder of the weekend with mostly clear skies.
