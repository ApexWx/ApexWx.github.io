3-Day Outlook: Monday - Wednesday

Severe thunderstorm watch in effect until 2 pm this afternoon

A fairly strong cold front trailing from a low pressure area in New York state will sweep east across the
Commonwealth late this morning through the mid afternoon hours. Scattered strong-to-severe storms, including discrete supercells, will develop and move through the Lancaster area in the 3-6 pm time frame based on current high-resolution forecast model data. Damaging wind gusts and isolated tornado activity is possible. Behind the front, winds will drop down and skies will clear as high pressure builds into the Lower Susquehanna Valley.

Tuesday will be mostly sunny and breezy with high pressure spreading across the Lancaster region.  Wednesday night looks to be clear with temperatures falling into the mid-40s overnight.

Wednesday will be partly cloudy with a slight chance for afternoon showers as a warm front lifts towards the region. Scattered showers are expected Wednesday night into Thursday morning as the front lifts northwards.
