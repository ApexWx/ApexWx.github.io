3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Canadian high pressure will build across the Valley today behind a backdoor cold front traversing the state northeast to southwest this morning. Temperatures will be below normal today and tonight with a frost advisory in effect overnight for northern Maine. Overnight lows in the lower 30s west to middle 30s east with some areas in the North Woods and around Estcourt Station seeing lows dip into the mid-to-upper 20s overnight.

Wednesday will be mostly sunny with temperatures closer to seasonal norms with highs in the mid-60s across much of the region. Wednesday night will feature mostly clear skies and light winds with lows dropping into the lower 40s for most Valley locations.

High pressure will remain across the SJV Thursday with mostly sunny skies and northerly winds. Thursday night will be mostly clear with a slight chance for showers as a cold front begins to approach from the west.
