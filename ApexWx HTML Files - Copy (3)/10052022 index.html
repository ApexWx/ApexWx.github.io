3-Day Outlook: Tuesday - Thursday

Some patchy fog is showing up in eastern portions of the Valley this morning as warm water vapor contacts the much colder air aloft. Fog will dissipate as the sun warms the air this morning. High pressure crests over the region today then moves south of the region. Sunny skies with highs in the lower 60s are expected in Fort Kent and vicinity. Tonight remains mostly clear, but not as cold with southerly flow maintaining overnight lows in the mid-to-upper 30s for much of the SJV.

Wednesday continues mostly sunny with highs approaching 70F in Fort Kent. Wednesday night remains mostly clear with southerly flow keeping overnight lows in the lower 40s.

High pressure will bring mostly sunny skies Thursday morning with increasing cloudiness in the afternoon as low pressure develops near the Great Lakes. This system will approach overnight into Friday bringing increasing chances for precipitation to the SJV.

4 to 7 Day: Friday - Monday

Low pressure approaches from the Great Lakes Friday pulling a cold front across the SJV. Showers are expected Friday afternoon into Saturday morning with showers lingering into Saturday afternoon as the associated upper-level low crosses the region.

Eastern Tropical Atlantic
A broad low pressure system located a few hundred miles southwest of the Cabo Verde Islands continues to produce a large area of showers and thunderstorms.  Environmental conditions appear to be conducive for further development, and a tropical depression is likely to form in a day or two.  Further development should become
less likely late this week due to increasing upper-level winds.  The system is forecast to move generally northwestward over the eastern tropical Atlantic.

East of the Windward Islands
A tropical wave located a few hundred miles east of the southern Windward Islands is producing disorganized showers and thunderstorms.  Some slow development of this system is possible during the next several days while the wave moves generally westward at 15 to 20 mph, reaching the Windward Islands and the eastern Caribbean Sea by midweek and the western Caribbean over the weekend. Interests in the Windward Islands should monitor the progress of this system.
