The Week Ahead for Fort Kent & the Upper Saint John Valley of Maine

⚠️ Current NWS Caribou Hazardous Weather Outlook ⚠️

7-Day Outlook: Tue. June 17 - Mon. June 23
Updated: Tuesday, June 17, 2025 at 8:12 am EDT

Tuesday - Thursday
High pressure will move away to the southeast today while low pressure tracks from the western Great Lakes to near James Bay in Canada and then into northern Québec today into Wednesday. A cold front associated with this system looks to stall out in Québec before reaching our region. Meanwhile, another low pressure area will track across the Great Lakes and track just northwest of the region Thursday into Friday pulling a cold front across the Valley Thursday night into early Friday.

Clouds increase today with a slight chance of showers by this afternoon into this evening. Scattered showers are possible overnight into Wednesday with a slight chance of showers Wednesday morning under mostly cloudy skies. Showers are likely with scattered thunderstorms possible for the area Wednesday afternoon with scattered showers continuing Wednesday night along with areas of fog.

Thursday, a chance of showers in the morning with mostly cloudy skies, then showers are likely with scattered thunderstorms Thursday afternoon. Models are not in complete agreement yet regarding the strength/speed of the cold front, but a stronger, faster-moving front could favor formation of a squall line with damaging winds the main threat. A slower-moving front would feature less wind potential. However, thunderstorms decrease in coverage Thursday evening with showers likely overnight into Friday morning. The Weather Predication Center has placed northern Maine at a marginal risk of excessive rainfall for Thursday.

Friday - Monday
Showers are likely Friday morning with a chance of showers lingering Friday afternoon and Friday night as an upper-level trough follows the surface system as it tracks east away from the SJV. High pressure builds in Saturday with a slight chance of showers lingering across the area. Sunday looks partly sunny with temperatures warming into the low 80s, and a slight chance of showers remains possible across the SJV.

Partly cloudy skies Sunday night and partly sunny skies Monday will have increasing chances of scattered afternoon showers and isolated thunderstorms as a warm front approaches leading to a warm day by Tuesday with temperatures in the middle 80s, based on current forecast model data.
